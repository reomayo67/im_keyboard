
I hade a pretty good base to start with today, the switch mounts were all in place, and all I had to do was
fuse together the plate, make a bottom and shell for the case, and boom id be done.
Originally, I had thought of making a topmounted plate, meaning that the keys wouldnt have any thing sorrounding them and would
just be sticking up from the keyboard, and its really easy

However, I just settled on making a shell that would make it look really good, and add more layers and hopefully stability to 
the keyboard.

I wanted to first make the outline of the shell, so i extruded cut out a hole from a part with the bottom of the pcb, split
and deleted all of the pillars made from the holes in the pcb, and had a indented block in which my pcb would fit.

As i was deciding how to do this, I gained inspo from my keyboard that has points around the keyboard where the shell can sit on top of
and that makes it look nice

SHELL DESIGN.

I started with the outline of the board that i got, and still had to shape the outside.

So after drawing a couple lines and filleting the corners I just eyeballed the amount for each corner and to what looked good in my eyes.
I also made a cutout for the MCU so it could be plugged in hassle free. Since it is allready raised by the through hole extender beam things it should be and issue, even without the cutout

.<img width="244" height="200" alt="Screenshot 2026-07-07 222321" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/b4a30d8d-bfe8-4836-83f3-996d4098c6ea" />

then I needed a way to mount the shell to the actuall board body itself. So i made some extensions from the inside of the shell, in which one overlaps with anoher screw hole. With that, I used the hole tool to make another counter sink, so I can just use the same hole but a longer screw,(abt 3 inches longher since thats how thiccc the extension is). With the other two, I just extended a long strip from the top mid of my shell and made their own screw holes, they go into the plate. In shich I will be mounting smaller screw inserts for.

<img width="538" height="326" alt="Screenshot 2026-07-07 222714" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/87f2fd10-73ee-4f0d-89ad-c42b3b2b7e6e" />

this Shell is partly inspired by my royale kludge R75, in white, which has a fillet indent going around the trim of the shell, and is what I attempted to recreate.

I just filleted the outside into a nice curve instead. the case is 3 mm higher than the plate, and shouldnt clip with any of the keys.
After that, I slightly scaled the shell part by like 1.001 to account for 3d print innacuracy, as before this thang was an airtight seal.
